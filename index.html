Welcome, I'm Leidy Arias!

As a child Leidy Arias enjoyed solving puzzles, solving math problems, and thinking about ways to innovate products around her. At an early age she began asking how things could be improved. With her curious mind and her drive to make a difference for those around her she graduated with a Computer Information Systems degree, enrolled in GW’s UI UX Bootcamp, and has had consulting experince; allowing her to develope a stong and diverse skill set to take on challenging User Experience roles. Ms. Arias combines research, prototyping, interaction and visual design to transform complex problems into elegant solutions. Innovating and or creating a product that improves our lifestyle provides Ms. Arias satisfaction and a sense of purpose.

   

My UX Skills

User Research

One of my favorite components of the Design Process is user research. The first part of the double dimond of design allows Product Managers to figure out what the true problem is. It allows me to see users pain points and reactions to everyday issues. By knowing the main deficiencies of a product, our team can solve the correct problem faced by the users. The preliminary user research, such as user interviews, surveys, and diary studies allow us to see preferences of the targeted user group and reveal ways to increase the value of our solution/ product. I enjoy preparing and user surveys and interviews as well as getting feedback from our targeted audience.

Ideation

The ideation phase allows Ms. Arias and the design team to translate the knowledge acquired during the User Research stage in the design thinking process and create a tangible model. Ms. Arias enjoys working with design teams to brainstorm ideas to create a large pool of solutions that can be filtered and evaluated later. While brainstorming with teams, Ms. Arias uses the I Like, I Wish, What if brainstorming tool that looks at ideas through the lens of the present, the near future, and the distant future. The method allows the team to be creative and think outside the box.

Analysis

As a Product Manager, Ms. Arias enjoys seeing the results of competitor analysis, and usability tests. Usability, making sure that the product works well is crucial and very important to her. A person of average ability and experience must be able to use the product for it's intended purpose easily. When thinking about the use of the teams creation, she focuses on the 5 Quality components: learnability, efficiency, memorability, errors, and satisfaction.
